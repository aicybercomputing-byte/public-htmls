Overview

Required applied experience for every tCAP student

Experiential learning is a required component of tCAP and gives students the opportunity to apply classroom knowledge in real-world environments.

Requirements

What every student must complete

  • Minimum threshold of 135 verified hours
  • Pre-approval of a plan of work and faculty mentor or quality supervisor
  • Develop and deliver a plan of work, mid-point update and final report with structured reflection
  • Assessment of pass/fail using a common rubric
  • Recorded as CIS 4950 (badge and transcript notation)
Eligible experiences

Approved pathways

Internships

Industry placements and supervised professional roles.

Undergraduate research

Research through USF Student Engagement in Research & Innovation.

Capstone projects

Applied academic projects tied to real deliverables.

Cybersecurity support

Cybersecurity support services with defined learning outcomes.

Entrepreneurship

Entrepreneurship initiatives and venture-building experiences.

Competitions & hackathons

Approved team-based technical competitions and hackathon events.

Study abroad

Global experiences that meet program criteria.

Approval process

How students get started

Students work with faculty mentors or approved supervisors to define goals, complete required documentation and track outcomes throughout the experience.
