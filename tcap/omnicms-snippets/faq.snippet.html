Program basics

What is the Tech Career Advancement Program (tCAP)?

The Tech Career Advancement Program is a structured, multiyear career development program integrated into the Bellini College experience. It combines career readiness, experiential learning and industry engagement to help students prepare for careers in AI, cybersecurity and computing.

How does tCAP help students prepare for careers in AI and cybersecurity?

tCAP helps students prepare for careers by integrating professional development, experiential learning and employer engagement throughout the student journey. Students build technical skills, professional competencies and real-world experience before graduation.

Is tCAP required for students at Bellini College?

tCAP is integrated into the student experience and includes required experiential learning components that support career readiness and professional development.

When do students start participating in tCAP?

Students begin participating in tCAP during their first semester, with programming that continues throughout their academic journey.

What does tCAP look like year by year?

tCAP follows a structured progression. Students build foundational skills early, develop professional experience in middle years and demonstrate mastery through applied projects and career preparation in later stages.

Experiential learning

What is experiential learning in tCAP?

Experiential learning is structured, hands-on experience that allows students to apply classroom knowledge in real-world settings. It is a core component of tCAP and supports skill development and career preparation.

How many hours of experience are required in tCAP?

Students are required to complete at least 135 verified hours of experiential learning through approved and supervised experiences.

What types of internships or experiences count for tCAP?

Eligible experiences include internships, undergraduate research, capstone projects, cybersecurity or IT support roles, entrepreneurship programs, hackathons, competitions and study abroad opportunities.

Can students complete more than one experiential learning activity?

Yes. Students may complete multiple experiential learning activities as long as they meet program requirements and contribute to their professional development.

What if I already have a job in tech? Does it count for tCAP?

Existing roles may qualify if they meet program requirements, include defined learning objectives and are approved by a faculty mentor or qualified supervisor.

Career support

Does tCAP help students get internships?

tCAP helps students prepare for internships through career coaching, resume and LinkedIn development, interview preparation and employer engagement. Students build relevant experience that makes them more competitive for internship opportunities.

What will I graduate with after completing tCAP?

Students graduate with a portfolio of applied work, professional and technical skills, industry connections and real-world experience that supports career readiness in technology fields.

Do students receive career support during tCAP?

Students receive ongoing support through career advisors, mentorship opportunities, networking events and employer engagement activities.

How do students connect with employers through tCAP?

Students connect with employers through networking events, mentorship programs, recruiting opportunities and applied project experiences.

How is tCAP different from just completing an internship?

tCAP provides a structured, multiyear framework that combines skill development, experiential learning and industry engagement. While internships are one component, tCAP ensures consistent career preparation across the entire student experience.

How does tCAP help students build a professional portfolio?

tCAP incorporates applied projects and experiential learning that allow students to document their work and demonstrate skills through a professional portfolio.

What skills do employers look for in AI and cybersecurity graduates?

Employers look for technical knowledge, communication skills, teamwork, problem-solving ability and the capacity to apply knowledge in real-world environments. tCAP is designed to help students develop these competencies.
