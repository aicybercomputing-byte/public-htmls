Experiential Learning

How It Works

Experiential learning is a required component of tCAP and gives students the opportunity to apply classroom knowledge in real-world environments.

1

Choose an experience

Select an eligible pathway such as an internship, research, capstone, hackathon or study abroad experience.

2

Get pre-approval

Pre-approval of a plan of work and faculty mentor or quality supervisor.

3

Complete the work

Meet the minimum threshold of 135 verified hours and deliver a mid-point update.

4

Reflect and record

Develop and deliver a final report with structured reflection, pass/fail assessment and CIS 4950 notation.

Eligible Experiences

Approved Pathways

Eligible experiences include internships, undergraduate research through USF Student Engagement in Research & Innovation, capstone projects, cybersecurity support services, entrepreneurship initiatives, competitions, hackathons and study abroad experiences.
